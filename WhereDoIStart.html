After getting a skateboard, you may want to first:


Once you feel comfortable cruising around and maybe even have learned a trick or two, you might feel ready to take on the big bad skate park. Riding around in one for the first time can be a nightmare, since it can be filled with intimidating teenagers and whatnot. You may find yourself ridden with anxiety and think everyone is looking at you and judging you, but knowing the proper etiquette, you can relieve yourself from a decent share of this.

Think of the Skate Park the Same Way You Would A Street:


- Look both ways before crossing and wait your turn. After going to said skate park regularly, you will learn where the unwritten lanes are.

- Don’t “snake” people. (snake, (v). taking your turn at the same time as somebody else and intercepting their path.) - When somebody’s board shoots out from under their feet towards you, you yell “board!”, stop their board, and return it to them.

This all may seem scary at first, and honestly, it will be. But at the end of the day, it is just another fear to conquer. Stress not though, since these rules are naturally learned over time. If you are a newbie who breaks one of these rules, people may be initially annoyed at first, but may be more understanding after you or something you do indicates that you are in fact new to skate parks. Another bottom line that goes back to a previous point: NOBODY CARES! Skateboarding is an individual thing, and people don’t gawk at you nearly as much as you think they do. Just skate.
